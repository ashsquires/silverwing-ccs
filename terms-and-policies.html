4.5 The Price Curve

4.5.1 The pricing structure for some products sold by Suppliers via the Platform is visible to Buyers as a graph showing price in pounds against the quantity of units sold. This graph is known as the Price Curve and is distinct for each relevant product available for purchase. For the avoidance of doubt not all products available for Buyers to purchase from Suppliers via the Platform will have an associated Price Curve.

4.5.2 Through the Platform a Buyer is able to purchase products in accordance with their Relevant Price Curve, where applicable.

4.5.3 Each Price Curve is only available during the Offer Period. Once the Offer Period has ended, no further purchases of that product will count toward the generation of Price Curve Credits under the Relevant Price Curve. For the avoidance of doubt, there is no limit to the number of subsequent offers that can be created for each product.

4.5.4 The Current Price of each Product at any given time within the Offer Period is dependent on the cumulative volume of units sold for that Product within all Qualifying Purchases, in accordance with the Relevant Price Curve.

4.5.5 As a Buyer inputs their specified quantity of a Product, their Price Payable updates in accordance with the Relevant Price Curve.

4.5.6 Upon acceptance of the relevant order, the Price Payable for that specific Order will be locked as the Price Due and the Current Price will for the Offer will be updated in real-time.

4.5.7 All prices are shown on the Relevant Price Curve excluding VAT.

4.5.8 In accordance with the Price Due and the Current Price and the pricing structure set out by the Price Curve under which a Qualifying Purchase was made, Price Curve Credits (see Section 4.7) will be displayed as a positive account balance on the Account.

4.7 Price Curve Credits

4.7.1 As a Buyer, should you make a Qualifying Purchase you will benefit from the collective of Qualifying Purchases made by all other Silverwing account holders under the same Price Curve.

4.7.2 Buyers will be able to see a positive balance on their Account (Price Curve Credits) where, in accordance with the Relevant Price Curve, the Current Price of the offer is less than the Price Due. The Price Curve Credits appearing on an Account will be equal to the difference between the Price Due less the Current Price. Price Curve Credits will continue to accrue on the Account until the Offer Period ends, at which point no further Price Curve Credits will accrue in relation to that Relevant Price Curve.

4.7.3 Price Curve Credits represent the savings on the Price Due that the Supplier has been able to pass onto Silverwing account holders, due to the collective impact of all Silverwing account holder purchases made against the Relevant Price Curve. The Price Curve Credits are provided at the sole discretion of the Supplier. You will receive a credit note from the Supplier to reflect the generation of Price Curve Credits. A reduction in the Current Price will instantly result in Price Curve Credits showing on the Accounts of all Silverwing account holders who made Qualifying Purchases under the Relevant Price Curve. The Buyer is able to view their Price Curve Credits on the Platform.

4.7.4 Price Curve Credits will show as Pending and will not be Redeemable for use by the Buyer until Silverwing has received payment in full of the Price Due.

4.7.5 It is the responsibility of the Buyer and Supplier to ensure appropriate accounting for VAT on any purchases made via the Platform and any Price Curve Credits you receive, in accordance with the Call Off Contract and all applicable laws, legislation and accounting standards.

4.7.6 In accordance with section 3.2 You authorise Silverwing to act as your agent for the purposes of receiving Redeemable Price Curve Credits from the Supplier. Redeemable Price Curve Credits will be held in trust on your behalf until your redemption, in accordance with section 4.8.

4.8 Redeeming Price Curve Credits

4.8.1 As a Buyer, you are entitled to have your Redeemable Price Curve Credits converted into a cash payment to be made to the bank details registered with us (‘Withdraw’/‘Withdrawal’), or to offset your Price Curve Credits against a future order, or orders through the Platform (‘Offset’/’Offsetting’).

4.8.2 You must Withdraw or Offset your Price Curve Credits within 24 months of the month in which the Offer Period, of the Relevant Price Curve relating to the product purchased, ended. If you do not redeem your Price Curve Credits either via a Withdrawal or via Offsetting, your Price Curve Credits will expire making them non-redeemable. This shall be the case regardless of when those Price Curve Credits became Redeemable. Price Curve Credits will expire after this time has lapsed and will no longer appear on your Account.

4.8.3 Price Curve Credits can be Offset across multiple purchases or against a single purchase through the Platform, as a partial or full offset of the value of the Price Payable for any purchase.

4.8.4 You may choose to Withdraw your Price Curve Credits. The Withdrawal may take up to ten (10) Business Days. For the avoidance of doubt, Silverwing will not fulfil requests to send such payments to any bank details that are not registered on your Account.

4.8.5 Silverwing reserves the right to forfeit your Price Curve Credits in full or in part, without notice or liability to you, in the following circumstances:

a) Where Silverwing is aware or has reason to believe that you have breached the Terms of this Agreement. This shall include, but is not limited to, where Price Curve Credits are due to be shown on an Account which has been:

I. suspended, disabled or terminated by us under section 3: ‘Terms of Use’ of this Agreement;

II. is suspected or known to be associated with fraudulent activity; and

III. in breach of section 3.5.1 used to make purchases on behalf of, or for the benefit of, any other person.

b) Where the Buyer has failed to pay a valid, undisputed Supplier or Silverwing invoice in full for a purchase from an Offer.

c) The Qualifying Purchase and/or order in question is cancelled after it has been entered into, for example where a purchased Product is returned or a right to cancel the transaction is exercised. This renders the purchase ‘Disqualified’.

d) for the avoidance of doubt any forfeit of Price Curve Credits under this section 4.8.5 will also be subject to the same escalation to CCS as described in section 3.7.
